Rules & Regulations

print Print

General

It is the responsibility of members to:

  1. Respect the chain of command
  2. Behave like a professional Roman soldier
  3. Follow the dress code at all times & stay in character
  4. Accept constructive criticism with grace & an open mind
  5. Be polite & respectful, you not only represent yourself but the group
  6. Be courteous and helpful to anyone hosting the legion
  7. Keep the venue clean & ensure everything is returned to it's original state before departing
  8. Have basic knowledge about what we reenact & answer questions as accurately as possible; Do not hesitate to admit when you don't know the answer & refer to someone else
  9. Posses the minimum amount of kit needed to participate (Let us know if you need something)
  10. Equipment is to be as period correct and functional as materials and costs permit
  11. All kit should be inspected for cleanliness and serviceability; see our maintenance page
  12. Absolutely no modern day paraphernalia is permitted (i.e., sunglasses, eyeglasses, wrist watches, piercings of any kind, eccentric hair colour, inappropriate visible tattoos, etc.)
  13. Some modern equipment (camping, food, etc.) is permitted after hours provided it is hidden during operational hours
  14. During public events all belongings should be secured by at least one member or an assistant

Dress Code

Legionaries

The following is a list of equipment needed to participate, depending on the weather & who you are portraying your requirements may be different. Check the equipment page for a full list:

Minimum

Around camp & public events
  • Tunica (Tunic) - A large shirt/dress held at the waist by a cord or cloth belt
  • Caligae (Sandals) - Modern leather sandals will do in a pinch
  • Balteus (Belt) - A leather ornamental apron used to affix weapons
  • Pugio (Dagger) - Attached to the Balteus

Battle Dress

During drill & demonstrations
  • Everything above
  • Cassis (Helmet) - To protect your head
  • Subarmalis (Padded Vest) - Worn under your armour for comfort and protection
  • Focale (Scarf) - Protects the neck from chaffing
  • Lorica (Armour) - Segmata, hamata or plumata/squamta
  • Gladius (Sword) - The primary weapon of the empire
  • Scutum (Shield) - A large curved shield
  • Pilum (Javelin) - A ranged throwing weapon

Non-Legionaries

Any re-enactors are welcome as long as they stay period correct for the 1st-2nd century AD. This could include civilians, barbarians & Greeks.

Weapons

The inappropriate use of weapons will not be tolerated:

  1. Be safe and conscious of yor weapons
  2. Use the verbal warning “Clear” before drawing weapons
  3. Always follow the instructions of the Range Safety Officer (RSO) when applicable
  4. Weapons are not to be handled by the public, under any circumstance
  5. Swords and daggers will be secured at all events with a device (like cord) to ensure that they cannot be removed by unauthorized personnel

Disciplinary Actions

Failure to act safely & professionally may result in the following disciplinary actions:

  • Verbal warnings
  • Removal of weapons
  • Expulsion from event
  • Suspension/discharge from legion
